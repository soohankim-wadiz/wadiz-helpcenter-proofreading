ImportantAre there any qualifications required to become a Wadiz maker?

와디즈 Share 🔗

📍 Worldwide ex-Korea Maker

Makers based outside Korea must meet certain qualifications to run a project on Wadiz. If you hold a Korean business license, you can run a Wadiz project the same way as a Korean maker.

1. Business Registration Country

Check the country where your business is registered. Only corporations and sole proprietors with a business registration certificate in one of the eligible countries below can become Wadiz makers:

  • Korea
  • United States
  • China
  • Hong Kong
  • Japan
  • Germany
  • Singapore
  • Poland
  • Australia

Eligible countries will be added over time. Manufacturers outside these countries can still browse Wadiz but cannot launch a project. For makers registered outside Korea, projects run as direct-purchase sales to the Korean and global markets.

2. Settlement Method by Country

Settlement is made through either Stripe Connect or PingPong Payments, depending on your business registration country.

Makers with a Chinese business license must have a PingPong Payments account to receive Wadiz settlements. With PingPong Payments, you receive settlement payments securely in USD and can withdraw them conveniently in your local currency, at the exchange rate Wadiz applies at the time the final settlement is transferred. Individuals without a registered business cannot join PingPong Payments, and therefore cannot run a project on Wadiz.

Makers with a business license outside China settle through Stripe Connect. Before opening a project, your identity verification (KYC) review via Stripe Connect must be approved in Maker Studio to schedule and open the project. To reduce exposure to exchange rate fluctuations, the USD amount is fixed based on the exchange rate published on the date the settlement statement is issued, and is automatically transferred to your local bank account.

3. No Separate Product Certification Required

You don't need to submit separate product certification documents. Manufacturers registered outside Korea don't need to register as a Korean business operator or mail-order business, and don't need to submit product certification documents (including KC certification) to run a project.
